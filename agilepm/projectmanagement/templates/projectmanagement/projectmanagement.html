Project Management

Find out about project management principles, themes and processes

Prince2 structure

There are three core elements to Prince2

  • Principles
  • Themes
  • Processes (the project lifecycle)
Themes and processes can be tailored to fit the project but principles should not be tailored.

Principles are guiding best practice and cannot be tailored. Principles are universal, self-validating and empowering. There are seven principles

  • Continued business justification
  • Learn from experience
  • Defined roles and responsibilities
  • Manage by stages
  • Manage by exception
  • Focus on products
  • Tailor to suit the project

There are seven themes

  • Business case (why)
  • Organisation (who)
  • Quality (what)
  • Plans (how much and when)
  • Risk (what if)
  • Change (what is the impact)
  • Progress (where are we going and should we carry on)

There are seven processes

  1. Starting a project
  2. Directing a project
  3. Initiating a project
  4. Controlling a stage
  5. Managing product delivery
  6. Managing a stage boundary
  7. Closing a project

Programmes and projects

A programme delivers strategic changes. A project delivers tactical changes. There are three principle project interests - business, user and supplier.
